Overview of Vigenere


The Vigenère cipher is a method of encrypting alphabetic text through a simple form of polyalphabetic substitution. It uses a keyword, where each letter of the keyword refers to a different Caesar cipher (shift). The strength of the Vigenère cipher comes from its use of multiple different Caesar ciphers in the encryption process, making it more secure than a simple Caesar shift cipher.

To encrypt using the Vigenère cipher, you first choose a keyword. Each letter of the plaintext message is then shifted along the alphabet by a number of places equal to the corresponding letter in the keyword. If the keyword is shorter than the message, the keyword is repeated to match the length of the message. For example, if the keyword is "KEY" and the message is "HELLO", the first letter H is shifted by the alphabet position of K (the first letter of the keyword), the second letter E is shifted by the position of E (the second letter of the keyword), and so on.
